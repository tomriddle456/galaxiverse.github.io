Sun

The Sun—a colossal ball of burning gases at the heart of our solar system. It's about 4.6 billion years old and is 109 times wider than Earth. Its core fuses hydrogen atoms into helium through nuclear fusion, releasing immense amounts of energy. This energy travels through the Sun's layers and radiates out as light and heat, which we feel here on Earth.
